Light Sources

Designing an Emotion Tracking App for Improved Mood Management

Light Source Limited is a personal development training organization that helps children and adults better manage their emotions and behavior. However, even with abundant assistance, understanding one's challenges is the first step toward improvement. This app achieves exactly that—helping people recognize their emotional patterns while recording daily events.

Overview

Challenge

The app is aim to track people's emotions, allowing them to record their thoughts and play music to comfort them. Additionally, there should be a section for sharing resources from the education center.

Opportunity

It is a great opportunity to enhance the overall learning experience for current members of the center, as well as an excellent way to attract new learners. I take a step back to understand how emotion management works and approach it in a holistic manner.

My Impact

I worked on this project as a solo designer alongside an engineer. I was responsible for the creation of all discovery and design deliverables, utilizing various online assets, such as illustrations, to enhance the design.

Design Process

Getting Started

There are many potential features for the app, taking into account both the requests from the education center and the possible needs of the users.






Wireframing

After filtering the features, I began creating a wireframe and communicating with the education center. The features and wireframe met their expectations, so I started to create a high-fidelity design.






Style Guide

The education center requested a yellow theme to indicate happiness. Therefore, I chose to use a bright yellow alongside some grey brown. Initially, black was considered as the button color; however, since black is traditionally viewed as "unlucky" in the Chinese community, we decided against using it.

User Journey

While designing the app, I gained knowledge on emotion management through online lessons taught by professional counselors. There are four steps in managing emotions:

1. Identify the emotion — recognize its existence and name it
2. Understand emotion — uncover the "why" behind it
3. Adjust emotion — maintain control
4. Express emotion — communicate effectively with words






Recognize its existence and Name it

We encounter a wide range of emotions, including joy, sadness, anger, anxiety, shame, and many others. Simply labeling a feeling as "bad" doesn't provide clarity. That's why identifying the specific emotion is one of the first steps in recording it. The learning centre has selected some of the most common emotions for users to choose from, and users can also add their own.






Uncover the “why” behind it

Now we need to understand why emotions appear, as sometimes their roots lie deeper than we might imagine. For example, anger toward someone could stem from an underlying fear. This app offers two ways to help.

1. Users can select what causes the emotion: When recording an emotion, users is recommended to select the category that triggered it. This helps them identify the initial cause of their reaction.

2. Emotion analysis: Another major feature of the app is the analysis report generated from users' emotion records. It shows the fluctuation of their moods, the percentage breakdown of how they feel, and what factors are most associated with those feelings.

Maintain control

If users want to express more, they can write a diary entry about their emotions. Putting thoughts into words helps clear their minds and creates space for clearer thinking. After recording their emotions and events, users can listen to soothing music and sound effects we've prepared, offering a brief escape from reality.

Communicate effectively with words

Beyond writing in a diary, the organization provides articles and training on emotion management and interpersonal skills. Users can access these valuable resources through the app by visiting the organization's website, helping them lead a more fulfilling life.

Design Challenges

How to record the intensity of emotion?

When users log their emotions, they start by selecting the intensity. We initially considered a scale ranging from -5 to 5, but we found that ten options were excessive. As a result, we decided to simplify the process by offering five distinct intensity levels instead.

How to show mood fluctuation?

In the generated report, we display mood fluctuation. Initially, we considered using bar charts to classify emotions into positive and negative groups. However, after consulting potential users, we decided not to emphasize "positive" or "negative" emotions. Instead, we switched to line charts to show the fluctuation, providing a more neutral representation of emotional changes over time.

Summary

This is a meaningful project aimed at helping people manage their emotions while also supporting the growth of the learning center. Throughout this process, I have learned a great deal about design and emotion management from professionals. In terms of design, it has been challenging to incorporate all features in a meaningful and user-friendly manner. After considerable effort in compositing and analyzing how other apps approach this, we have finally completed the app, and the education center is pleased with the design!
